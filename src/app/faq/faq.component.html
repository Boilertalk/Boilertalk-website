What is a chatbot?

We work with two different kinds of chatbots. On the one hand, we develop messenger chatbots for Telegram and Facebook messenger. These are little Applications inside an existing messenger. The difference to regular mobile apps is that users don't have to download anything to use the app, as they communicate directly with a server, inside the user interface of the messenger. On the other hand we sometimes integrate chatbots into our websites. These are the little windows popping up in some websites that say something like: "Hello, can I help you?". These are often very useful marketing tools to increase the user conversion rate.

What is a smart contract?

Smart contracts are programs that are used to verify or enforce a defined contract wihtout the need for any third parties. It is similar to an automatically enforced contract between people which, once subscribed, can't be ever broken. This is possible as people all over the world are witnesses who are paid a small fee, if and only if they enforce the contract contents to be performed properly. Because of the basics of game theory we know that the case where more than 50% of all people involved disagree to receive their money is statistically impossible. Consider reading this very interesting paper regarding game theory in economics: https://econpapers.repec.org/bookchap/elgeebook/587.htm

What is a miner?

To process (verify) transactions, cryptocurrencies need a dezentralized network of so called "mining nodes". Smart contracts, which we discussed earlier, are a good example of what a "transaction" can be. The validity and integrity of a smart contract execution are checked thouroughly by miners to make a dezentralized network possible. These miners check the validity of the transactions by performing complicated cryptographic calculations that need a lot of computational power. For doing so, they receive a reward. As it takes very long for an individual to get to the point of a single reward, individual miners split the work between them, using a platform called "mining pool". These pools provide the infrastructure on which the users can mine and in return take a small fee of the reward. We are offering a pool for monero mining and provide a miner that runs inside the browser for website owners. For use of our pool ( which is set by default), we take a small fee to keep our servers up and running. The miner is completely free to use.
